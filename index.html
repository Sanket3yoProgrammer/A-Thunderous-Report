Filed during a thunderstorm in Jharsuguda, Odisha, by one Class 12 PCM student with too much free time. What follows is 19 factors, each one narrowing the odds, ending in a number so small it stops being about physics at all.

01 / POWER

Phone power vs. lightning power

P_t ≈ 1 W    P_l = VI ≈ (10⁸)(3×10⁴) ≈ 3×10¹² W
P_t / P_l ≈ 3.3 × 10⁻¹³

The cloud outguns the phone by 12 orders of magnitude. It is not a fair fight.

02 / POISSON

Building strike rate

λ = N_g · A = (10)(10⁻⁴) = 10⁻³ / yr
P(1) = λe⁻λ ≈ 9.99 × 10⁻⁴

About a 1-in-1000 chance the building gets struck in any given year.

03 / GEOMETRY

Height competition (electrogeometric model)

R ∝ H^0.6  →  R_b=3.21, R_t=5.30, R_p=3.98
F_b = R_b² / ΣR² = 10.30 / 54.23 ≈ 0.190

My building draws 19% of the local lightning's attention. The tree gets the rest.
